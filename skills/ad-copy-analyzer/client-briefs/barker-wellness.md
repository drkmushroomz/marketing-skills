# Barker Wellness — Client Context Brief

## Brand Identity
Celebrity-founded wellness/CBD brand by **Travis Barker** (Blink-182). Associated with **Courtney Kardashian**. Young brand, premium "better for you" positioning. DTC-first, expanding retail. Travis's cult following is the primary differentiator.

## Product Portfolio
| Category | Key SKUs | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Gummies (Hero)** | Daytime (Mushroom Adaptogen), Nighttime, Immunity + Vitamin, Vitamin D, B Complex, Focus | Nighttime outperforms daytime. Vitamin D = 30% of Google gummy conversions. Immunity ad was historical top performer. |
| **Topicals** | Muscle Therapy Cream/Balm, Tattoo Balm | Tattoo balm = high opportunity (tied to Travis identity). Muscle balm has had stock issues. "Courtney Collection" exists. |
| **Bundles/Duos** | Various | 10-16% built-in discounts. Underexplored in ads. Key AOV lever. |
| **Subscriptions** | All products | LTV tracking as ideal attribution model. |

## Pricing & Promotions
- DTC: ~$35/product. Free shipping over $100 (free for first-time customers).
- Amazon: ~$30 with Prime shipping (undercutting DTC).
- Active: **25% off first-time code** (outperforms 40% off in CTR + CVR).
- **Rule of 100 note:** Products under $100, so always use % off (not $ off).
- Upsell feature live on all PDPs. Discount stacking enabled.

## Target Audience
- **Primary personas:** "Skeptical switchers," white-collar professionals, amateur athletes, wellness seekers.
- **Psychographics:** Natural daily rituals, self-directed health, ingredient transparency, benefit-driven purchasing.
- **80% mobile traffic.** 50% of conversions happen Friday-Sunday.

## Competitive Landscape
- Amazon is direct price competitor. DTC must differentiate via exclusives, founder story, bundles.
- **Four Sigmatic (Sigmatic)** = UX/brand benchmark.
- Competitor gaps found via Reddit reviews: sugar-free options, "edible moments."

## Brand Voice & Messaging
**Content Pillars:**
1. Wellness & Lifestyle — athletic, daily rituals, natural living
2. Ingredients — vegan, natural, specific benefits (mushroom adaptogens, CBD, vitamins)
3. Problem-Solution — cognitive function, relaxation, recovery, sleep, soreness

**Hierarchy:** Benefits first, ingredients second. Never lead with ingredients alone.

**Brand Reassurance:** Travis Barker = trust signal. Clinician testimonials + reviews for mid-funnel. Address FUDs: satisfaction guarantees, return policies, shipping, site security.

**Tone:** Premium but accessible. Not clinical. Athletic and aspirational. Lifestyle upgrade, not medical intervention.

## Creative Strategy — What Works
- Product displayed with **nature backgrounds**
- **Benefit-led messaging** + athletic lifestyle targeting "skeptical switchers"
- Static catalog > slideshow catalog
- Problem-solution angle + ingredient benefits framework
- Travis's face/likeness as attention capture
- Nighttime campaign creative (after pruning non-converters)
- 25% off offer (outperforms 40% off)
- Weekend-heavy budget (+25% Fri-Sun, -20% Mon-Thu)

## Creative Strategy — What Doesn't Work
- Low creative diversity → ad fatigue
- Vague headlines without benefit support ("recovery fits into real life")
- Hooks without ingredient/benefit follow-through ("I don't wait until I'm sore" — but why?)
- Low-arousal words only (calm, gentle, soothing) without high-arousal hooks
- PMax campaigns driving irrelevant traffic
- Current PDP design hurting conversion (0.38% CVR)

## Strategic Rules for Ad Copy
1. Medical/health claims must be client-approved. Don't make unapproved claims.
2. Benefits first, ingredients second.
3. Travis Barker should appear in creative (no speaking videos yet, only product footage).
4. Website is a known conversion bottleneck — CRO overhaul underway.
5. Blended attribution: Meta starts journey, Google closes.
6. Weekend messaging and urgency are effective.
7. DTC copy should emphasize what Amazon can't: exclusivity, bundles, first-time offers.
8. Ad fatigue is active risk — diversify emotion, angle, format, funnel stage.
9. Remarketing copy should push subscriptions.
10. **Tattoo balm** is underexplored with strong brand-identity fit (Travis = tattoos).
